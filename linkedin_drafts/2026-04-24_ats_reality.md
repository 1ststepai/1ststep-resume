75% of resumes never reach a human.

Not because they're bad. Because the ATS parsed them, scanned for keywords from the job description, and filed them under "not a match."

Here's what's actually happening behind the scenes:

The system pulls specific terms from the job post — tools, certifications, skill phrases. Your resume either has them in the exact phrasing or it doesn't. "Managed AWS infrastructure" is not the same as "Amazon Web Services" to most parsers.

Two quick fixes that move the needle:

1. Mirror the job post's language. If they wrote "stakeholder management," use that — not "cross-functional collaboration."
2. Put your top 3 hard skills in the summary at the top, not buried on page 2.
